The Diffrent Types of Golf Clubs

  • Woods
  • Irons
  • Hybrids
  • Wedges
  • Putters

Woods

The woods clubs consist of the driver and the fairway woods. Although they are called woods their clubheads are no longer made from wood. The reason to pick a club from the woods category would be because golfers are able to swing them the fastest and are used for long shots from the teeing ground.

These are usually peoples favorite, not only becasue of the long range but, also how satisfying a perfect stike sounds.

Irons

Irons come in numbered sets ranging from 3-iron to 9-iron. Irons have smaller clubheads than woods and are usually very thin. Irons are generally used on shots from the fairway. The lower the iron number the further the ball should travel

Hybrids

Hybrids are a newer category of golf clubs. Just like the name itself, it is a hybrid between the woods and the irons. Just like the irons they are numbered, and the number corresponds to the iron they replace.

Wedges

The wedges category includes the pitching wedge, gap wedge, sand wedge and lob wedge. Wedges are their own type of golf club. Wedges are used for shorter shots into the greens, pitches around the green and are very helpful for playing out of sand traps.

Putters

Putters comes in the widest varieties of shapes and sizes. Putters are used for putting in the putting greens to make the ball into the hole.
